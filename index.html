The problem

Brain metastases create a uniquely difficult therapeutic environment.

Effective mRNA therapeutics must overcome physical, molecular, and immunological barriers that limit drug delivery and durable anti-tumor response.

🧠
Barrier 01

Blood-Brain Barrier

The BBB restricts therapeutic access and complicates systemic delivery, requiring delivery designs that balance penetration, specificity, and safety.

🧬
Barrier 02

Tumor Heterogeneity

Brain metastases vary across patients, lesions, and clonal populations, demanding adaptive target selection rather than one-size-fits-all design.

❄️
Barrier 03

Cold Immune Niche

The brain metastatic microenvironment can suppress immune activation, reducing the effectiveness of conventional immunotherapeutic strategies.

Stop & Go conjugate

A dual-function therapeutic logic: suppress what protects the tumor, express what activates immunity.

The conceptual platform combines a STOP module and a GO module to create an integrated therapeutic sequence.

Stop = PNA

Peptide Nucleic Acid Suppression Logic

The PNA arm is proposed as a selective inhibitory element designed to interfere with tumor-supportive or immune-evasive transcripts relevant to brain metastatic survival.

Go = mRNA

mRNA Immune Activation Logic

The mRNA arm is designed to express selected antigens, immune modulators, or therapeutic payloads that promote recognition and anti-tumor response.

1

Target Recognition

Patient and tumor data define candidate neoantigens, immune-evasion pathways, and biologically relevant intervention points.

2

Dual Payload Delivery

A delivery strategy transports the therapeutic construct toward brain metastatic tissue while prioritizing stability and safety.

3

Immune Reprogramming

PNA-mediated suppression and mRNA-mediated expression work together to shift the lesion from immune-cold toward immune-visible.

Validation roadmap

Three phases, one continuous feedback loop.

The roadmap moves from computational design to experimental validation and back into model improvement.

I

Digital Feasibility

Build the data schema, define candidate targets, create computational scoring pipelines, and generate the first therapeutic design dossier.

II

In Vitro Validation

Test expression, knockdown or suppression logic, immune activation markers, cell viability, and reproducibility in relevant model systems.

III

Translational Readiness

Integrate results into improved design rules, prioritize lead constructs, and prepare for funding, partnership, and advanced preclinical work.

Experimental Results → Model Refinement → Improved Construct Design

Every validation result becomes training intelligence for the next sprint of the pipeline.
